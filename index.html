Our Mission

Every Child Deserves the Tools to Learn

"Things laying around as 'spare' or 'old' can make a world of difference in someone else's life."

Vision for All began with a simple observation during a 2024 teaching visit to Sharp Memorial School for the Blind in Rajpur, India: students lacked basic school supplies that most of us take for granted.

What started as one teenager's mission has grown into a community effort—collecting backpacks, school supplies, and educational materials from Long Island families and transporting them thousands of miles to students who need them most.

The Journey

From Classroom to Community

Every great initiative starts with a single step. Here's how Vision for All evolved from a personal experience into a growing movement.

July 2024

The Spark: Teaching in India

Volunteered at Sharp Memorial School for the Blind, teaching mathematics, English, geography, and environmental studies across multiple grades. Witnessed firsthand the challenges students face with limited resources.

July 2025

First Backpack Drive

Launched the inaugural backpack collection campaign across Long Island. Collected approximately 100 used and new backpacks from generous community members who understood that their "extras" could transform a student's education.

December 2025

Delivery & Distribution

Traveled to Sharp Memorial School to personally deliver the collected backpacks. Met with school staff and distributed supplies to students on campus during the holiday season.

2026 & Beyond

Growing the Movement

Expanding beyond one person. Building a network of youth volunteers, launching new initiatives including braille book drives and remote tutoring programs, and creating replicable "chapter" models for other schools.

About Mihir

Mihir Goyal is a high school student from Long Island, New York, passionate about education equity and community service. Inspired by a 2024 teaching experience in India, he founded Vision for All to bridge the gap between resources and need.

What began as a personal mission has become a call to action for peers, families, and communities to recognize that small contributions create massive impact.

About Sharp Memorial School

Sharp Memorial School for the Blind, located in Rajpur, India, serves both visually impaired and sighted students in an inclusive learning environment. The school provides education with limited technological resources but unlimited dedication.

Students here often lack basic supplies like backpacks, notebooks, and writing materials—items that can make the difference between falling behind and thriving.
